I'm Leaving CKS

So, you've heard the news... I'm leaving CKS
This blog entry will hopefully clarify what exactly that means.
CKS was a small team of people, joined together by Chawi Mi. The team dates as far back as early 2018 (as far as I know), and I was the last member invited while Chawi was around.
The team mainly consisted of 8 members, but the whole og team can be found here (idk what the site looks like in 2022, the last time I looked it was 2019)

Back to why I left;
I left CKS because the team isn't what it used to be. When Chawi was around, the team was both more active and more productive, a friendly-environment.
After Chawi passed the team kinda fell apart.

It was fun, we all had a nickname over our other nicknames, three letters and a number, so that we all received credit, and none of us at the same time.
We remained anonymous to even each-other.
As a matter-of-fact, we didn't introduce ourselves to one another until after Chawi passed.
We made some weird projects, such as the now defunct CKS-E.P. (CKS Editing Program), SYSTEM-O-M4T1C and some other poggy McPog things.
But for me, the best memories made are now completely wiped from existence.

Chawi was a rich guy, who didn't mind sharing, who anyone could rely on, who was there when you needed him.
That said, my favorite parts of the CKS Dev Team, the CKS-E team, the CKS Digital Works team and the CKS mixing group died with him.
Chawi was rather tech-savvy, and knew how to protect his files. Some of my favorite things we worked on are saved on an external hard-drive that Chawi had, formatted beyond belief.
V1RU5 attempted to recover a lot of the data Chawi had stored around, including, and not limited to HiT World, the file server for SYStemware, and SYSbot, but there is still loads of formatted or corrupt data.

These are my personal reasons for leaving the CKS Team. I hope they don't affect the other members of the team's decisions, or how you, the reader, thinks of me.
